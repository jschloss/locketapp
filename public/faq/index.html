Basics

  1. How does Locket work?

    Locket is a pair of free apps: one for your iPhone, and one for your Mac. The two apps talk to eachother via Bluetooth Low Energy to provide secure, automatic sign-in to your Mac.

    When you approach your Mac, your iPhone uses Bluetooth Low Energy to tell your Mac that you're there. After a few security checks, your Mac tells your iPhone to vibrate.

    When your iPhone vibrates, tap it twice with your hand. Locket will then automatically enter your password in a tenth of a second.

  1. What is Bluetooth Low Energy?

    Bluetooth Low Energy is the newest version of Bluetooth, a short-range wireless networking system found in things like computers, phones and even televisions. Sometimes people call it Bluetooth 4.0. Essentially, Bluetooth Low Energy does all the things regular old Bluetooth does, but uses a heck of a lot less power.

Compatibility

  1. What do I need to use Locket?

    To use Locket, you'll need two things:
    1) An iPhone 4S or iPhone 5
    2) A Mac that supports Bluetooth Low Energy.

  1. Which Macs support Bluetooth Low Energy?

    MacBook Pro: 2011 or newer.
    MacBook Air: 2011 or newer.
    Mac Mini: 2012 or newer.
    iMac: 2013 or newer.

Security

  1. Is Locket really more secure than a password?

    You bet. Locket offers all the security of a strong password, plus protection from bad guys reading your password as you type, malicious software recording your password, and unwanted access to your computer when you leave it unattended.

  2. What if someone steals my iPhone?

    When using Locket, you can still sign in to your Mac with your password. If someone steals your iPhone, simply sign in with your password and unpair your phone.

  3. What if someone steals my iPhone and my Mac?

    Yikes, talk about a rough day. Even in the case that a thief makes off with both your iPhone and your Mac and is aware that your Mac is protected with Locket, you can easily wipe your iPhone using iCloud to prevent the thief from gaining access to your Mac. (You'll want to do that anyway, even if you don't use Locket.)

  1. What if my iPhone is protected by a password?

    Your Locket will unlock your Mac even if your iPhone is locked.

  2. Can Locket be fooled into giving my password to a malicious application?

    No. Locket verifies the 'code signature' of your Mac's login system to make sure that it and only it can access your password.

  3. What if someone near me is also using Locket?

    No worries. Your Mac only recognizes your Locket.

  4. Can't someone hack the Bluetooth connection between Locket and my Mac?

    Locket sends your password over Bluetooth using military grade 2048-bit RSA encryption. Even if a hacker were to intercept your Bluetooth signal, your password would appear as an encrypted jumble.

Privacy

  1. I see Locket uses my iPhone's GPS location. Why?

    We very occasionally check your iPhone's GPS location as a clever way to help save your iPhone's battery. We've written a nice little algorithm that predicts when you will use Locket, and only turns on your Bluetooth chip when Locket needs it.

  1. Do you ever record my GPS data?

    We do record certain locations to help our algorithm, but we never send that data to a server. It stays safe right on your iPhone. And let's make one thing absolutely clear: we will never ever record or share any personally identifiable information.
